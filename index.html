With nearly two decades of experience, Minha Chung is a seasoned public policy executive operating at the intersection of technology, regulation, and corporate strategy. As the former Chief Policy Officer (CPO) at Naver, Korea's leading technology company, he spearheaded the company's government affairs and public policy strategy. In this role, he navigated complex legislative processes, shaped the public narrative on key industry issues, and built a trusted, high-level network within the National Assembly and regulatory bodies. His successful defense against the 2008 'Search Service Provider Bill' is a key example of his long-standing commitment to advocating for global standards against overreaching regulation. Seeking to understand policy from the ground up, he later co-founded a startup in the highly regulated PropTech sector. This entrepreneurial journey equipped him with a rare dual perspective—that of both an established market leader and a nimble innovator. Minha Chung is a firm believer in building a predictable and innovation-friendly regulatory environment. His work is guided by a principled stance on fair competition, leveraging his deep expertise and strategic acuity to act as a bridge between the technology industry and policymakers.
